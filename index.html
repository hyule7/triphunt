💡 Flight Booking Tips

Insider knowledge to save you more
📅
Book Tuesday–Wednesday
Airlines typically release sale fares on Tuesdays. Searching on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning often finds the lowest prices before they get snatched up.
🗓️
6–8 Weeks is the Sweet Spot
For European short-haul flights, booking 6–8 weeks ahead usually hits the best balance between availability and price. Too early can mean paying peak-launch fares.
🔔
Set a Price Alert
Click 🔔 Alert on any flight result. We'll email you the moment prices drop below your target. Free, no account needed.
🏙️
Check Nearby Airports
Flying from Stansted instead of Heathrow can save £40+. Always check multiple UK departure airports for the same route — the price difference can be significant.
⚡
Direct vs 1-Stop
For long-haul, a 1-stop with a sensible connection can be 30–40% cheaper. Use TripHunt filters to compare direct vs 1-stop side-by-side.
🧳
Baggage Can Double the Price
Budget airlines advertise fares without bags. Use our Baggage Calculator to find the true total cost before comparing. Sometimes a "more expensive" airline is cheaper all-in.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How does TripHunt make money?+
We earn a small affiliate commission from our booking partners (like Aviasales, Booking.com, Klook) when you click through and make a booking. This never adds to your cost — you always pay the same price as booking directly. TripHunt is completely free to use.
Are the prices shown accurate and up to date?+
Yes — flight prices are fetched from the TravelPayouts API in real time. Prices shown are indicative and confirmed at checkout on the partner's site. Prices can change minute-to-minute, so always confirm before booking.
Do I book through TripHunt or directly with the airline?+
You click through from TripHunt to the partner site (e.g. Aviasales/JetRadar) and book directly there. TripHunt never takes payment. Your booking confirmation comes from the partner, not TripHunt.
What is the cheapest day to fly?+
Generally, Tuesday and Wednesday departures are cheapest for short-haul European routes. Friday and Sunday evenings are typically the most expensive. For long-haul, the day of the week matters less than the season.
How far in advance should I book flights?+
For European short-haul: 6–10 weeks ahead is typically the sweet spot. For long-haul (USA, Asia, Australia): 3–5 months ahead. Last-minute deals do exist but are increasingly rare and unreliable.
What currencies does TripHunt use?+
All prices on TripHunt are shown in GBP (£). We're built specifically for UK travellers.
