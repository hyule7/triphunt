💡 Pro Booking Tips
Insider knowledge that saves UK travellers money every time
▼
01
Book Tuesday or Wednesday
Airlines typically release sales on Monday nights. By Tuesday–Wednesday, competitors have matched them - meaning you see the lowest prices.
02
6–8 Weeks Before is the Sweet Spot
For European routes, prices are lowest 6–8 weeks out. Long-haul (US, Asia) works best 3–5 months ahead. Booking too early can cost more.
03
Fly Mid-Week to Save Up to 30%
Tuesday, Wednesday and early Thursday departures are consistently cheaper than Friday and Sunday flights on the same route.
04
Use Nearby Airports
Gatwick vs Heathrow, Stansted vs Luton - the same destination can vary by £60–£120 depending on which London airport you depart from.
05
Pre-Pay Bags Online - Always
Ryanair and easyJet charge up to 3x more for bags at the airport gate than online. Add baggage at booking or via Manage My Booking.
06
Clear Cookies Before Booking
Some booking sites use dynamic pricing based on your browsing history. Use a private/incognito window when checking prices to avoid inflated fares.
